What's Mental Health?

and why is it important?

image via @freepik

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC, 2020), “Mental illnesses are conditions that affect a person’s thinking, feeling, mood or behavior, such as depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, or schizophrenia. Such conditions may be occasional or long-lasting (chronic) and affect someone’s ability to relate to others and function each day.” 
 
The CDC states that the factors of Mental health include our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. Our Mental health affects how we think, feel, and act.  Further, it also helps to determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices. Because Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood, it is important that we check-in with our overall emotional and behavioral health.  This process can begin with your local primary physician, a behavioral health expert, and self-awareness of your emotional health (CDC, November 2020). 


Both the terms Mental Illness and Mental health are often used interchangeably; however, one needs to understand that poor mental health and mental illness are not the same things. A person can experience poor mental health and not be diagnosed with a mental illness. Likewise, a person diagnosed with a mental illness can experience periods of physical, mental, and social well-being (CDC, November, 2020). 
 
The CDC (2020), states that Mental and physical health are equally important components of overall health.  Mental illness, especially depression, increases the risk for many types of physical health problems, particularly long-lasting conditions like stroke, type 2 diabetes, and heart disease. Similarly, the presence of chronic conditions can increase the risk for mental illness.

Read/Watch/Listen

how we're learning more about mental health

image via @freepik

  • "Mental Health - Why It’s Important for Students and Teachers Alike” by Elise Morgan  
    • This article showcases the significance in understanding how our mental health impacts our overall wellbeing. From students to teachers, both parties are likely to experience mental health issues from on semester to another. 
  • “The Struggle Bus” Podcast by Katherine Heller and Sally Tamarkin 
    • Two best friends, Katherine Heller and Sally Tamarkin, sit down and offer advice on topics such as mental health to scientology. This podcast answers questions from listeners and are said to “have lots of feelings and opinions”.  
  • “Stop the Stigma: Why It’s Important to Talk About Mental Health” by Heather Sarkis 
    • Heather Sarkis is a Psychiatric-Mental Health Nurse Practitioner who addresses the stigma behind the topic of mental health, how mental health issues are commonly misinterpreted, and how mental health issues are typically misrepresented in our societies. In this TED talk, Sarkis highlights the urgency and immediate need for us to act on our mental health the same way that we take our annual physical exams.
